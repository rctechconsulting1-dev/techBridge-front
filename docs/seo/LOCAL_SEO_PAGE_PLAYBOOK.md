# Local SEO Page Playbook

Status: Working playbook
Owner: RD Tech Bridge
Purpose: Reusable guidance for filling built-in pages so they rank and convert.

## What this is

This file is the operational knowledge base for SEO-focused page filling in this repo.

The goal is not to sound "SEO optimized." The goal is to create pages that:

1. Match local search intent.
2. Clarify what the business does and where.
3. Build trust fast.
4. Convert visitors into calls, forms, bookings, or purchases.

## Core principles

1. One page, one main job.
Home should clarify offer + location + next action.
Services should explain offers and help visitors find the right fit.
About should build trust and credibility.
Shop should support commercial intent and product discovery.

2. Search intent first, not word count.
If the page does not answer what a searcher wants in the first screenful, ranking copy later on the page will not save it.

3. Facts beat adjectives.
Prefer concrete inputs like service type, city, hours, response speed, license status, specialties, and process over vague claims.

4. Conversion clarity is part of SEO.
If the page ranks but the CTA is weak or mismatched, the page is underperforming.

5. Local specificity matters.
Use real service areas and local context when available. Do not invent neighborhoods, cities, or claims.

## Home page framework

Use the Home page to answer these questions immediately:

1. What does the business do?
2. Who is it for?
3. Where does it operate?
4. What should the visitor do next?

### Home page checklist

1. Hero title names the business or core offer clearly.
2. Hero body includes service type + geography + core outcome.
3. Primary CTA matches the conversion mode.
4. CTA text is direct: Call Now, Request Service, Get a Free Estimate, Book an Appointment.
5. SEO title includes business name + service/category + geography when useful.
6. SEO description summarizes offer + geography + trust/action signal in plain language.

### Good Home formulas

Hero body formula:
Reliable [service type] in [city/region]. We help [customer type] with [top problems/offers] using [proof/process if known].

SEO title formula:
[Business Name] | [Primary Service] in [City]

SEO description formula:
[Business Name] provides [service type] in [city/region], including [2-3 key offers]. [CTA or trust signal].

## Services page framework

Use the Services page to help the visitor self-sort quickly.

### Services page checklist

1. Hero intro explains the service scope, not generic brand fluff.
2. Service list names real offers using customer language.
3. Empty-state copy is not the final copy if real services exist.
4. CTA matches the real next step: call, estimate, booking, or contact.
5. Copy naturally reinforces service categories and problems solved.

### Good Services intro formula

We provide [broad category] for [customer type] in [service area], including [service 1], [service 2], and [service 3].

## About page framework

Use the About page to reduce trust friction.

### About page checklist

1. Explain who the business is and why it exists.
2. Add founder, team, process, or credibility details when available.
3. Support claims with specifics, not slogans.
4. End with a clear trust-to-contact path.

### Good About formula

[Business Name] helps [customer type] with [service category] in [region]. We focus on [differentiator], [process], and [result].

## CTA guidance by conversion mode

### Call
- Best buttons: Call Now, Speak With Us, Request Service
- Best URLs: tel links when the business wants direct calls

### Email or lead form
- Best buttons: Get a Free Estimate, Request a Quote, Contact Us
- Best URLs: #contact or the actual form route

### Appointment
- Best buttons: Book an Appointment, Schedule Now, Reserve Your Spot

### Checkout
- Best buttons: Shop Now, Browse Products, Start Checkout

## Trust signals to look for

Use these if they exist in intake or settings:

1. License or certification
2. Insurance status
3. Years in business
4. Service area
5. Reviews or Google Business presence
6. Before/after or project photos
7. Team or founder identity
8. Clear process or response-time promise

## What not to do

1. Do not repeat the same city unnaturally.
2. Do not pad pages with empty mission language.
3. Do not invent trust factors.
4. Do not use weak CTA text like Learn More when the goal is leads.
5. Do not treat SEO title and meta description as optional.

## Missing-input prompts

If ranking copy is weak because the business context is thin, ask for or flag these exact items:

1. Top 3 services
2. Primary city or service area
3. Ideal customer type
4. Biggest differentiator
5. Preferred lead action
6. Proof signals: license, insurance, years, reviews, certifications

## Proven patterns

1. For local lead-gen businesses, Home works best when the hero body includes service category, geography, and a direct action path in one short paragraph.
2. Call-driven businesses should usually avoid soft CTA text like Learn More on Home.
3. About pages perform better when they translate brand story into trust, not autobiography.

## Business-type playbooks

### Electricians and home service trades

Primary intent usually includes:

1. electrician near me
2. electrical services in [city]
3. electrical repair in [city]
4. panel upgrade, wiring, troubleshooting, lighting, outlets, EV charger, or commercial electrical terms when those are real offers

What to emphasize:

1. Residential and commercial scope if true
2. Fast response, repairs, troubleshooting, upgrades, and installations if true
3. License, insurance, and service area if known
4. Clear call or estimate CTA

What to avoid:

1. Generic hero copy with no electrical context
2. Soft CTA language on call-first sites
3. Inventing emergency or same-day claims

Suggested Home formula:
[Business Name] provides reliable electrical services in [city/region], including [offer 1], [offer 2], and [offer 3].

### Plumbers and HVAC companies

Primary intent usually includes:

1. plumber in [city]
2. HVAC service in [city]
3. drain cleaning, water heater, AC repair, heater repair, maintenance, installation

What to emphasize:

1. Repair plus install scope
2. Service area clarity
3. Urgency only if explicitly supported
4. Estimate or call CTA

### Med spas, wellness, and appointment businesses

Primary intent usually includes:

1. med spa in [city]
2. facials, injectables, massage, therapy, or treatment-specific terms in [city]
3. book appointment and consultation intent

What to emphasize:

1. Treatment categories and ideal client problems
2. Booking clarity
3. Practitioner credentials and process if known
4. Clean, reassuring trust language

What to avoid:

1. Medical-sounding claims the business has not provided
2. Generic spa fluff without treatment specificity

### Personal trainers and fitness coaches

Primary intent usually includes:

1. personal trainer in [city]
2. fitness coach in [city]
3. weight loss coach, strength coach, online coaching, private training

What to emphasize:

1. Training type: one-on-one, group, virtual, in-home, gym-based
2. Specialties: weight loss, strength, athletic performance, mobility, etc.
3. Consultation or trial CTA
4. Certifications if known

What to avoid:

1. Vague motivation-only copy with no service specifics
2. Inventing transformation claims or results

### Local retail and ecommerce

Primary intent usually includes:

1. product category terms
2. branded search terms
3. local shop intent when pickup or local service exists

What to emphasize:

1. Product categories and shopper fit
2. Discovery and merchandizing clarity
3. Shipping, pickup, or local availability when real
4. Strong browse/shop CTA

## Onboarding workflow

Use this order when filling built-in pages during onboarding:

1. Home first: clarify the business, geography, and main CTA.
2. Services second: name real offers and support internal linking.
3. About third: strengthen trust and credibility.
4. Shop only when ecommerce is active for the tenant.
